This notepad expands on the high-level outcomes and measurement bullet points

SECTION 1 — TARGET OUTCOMES (Detailed Explainability)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1.1  Reduce per-call QA time from ~30 minutes to < 5 minutes within 90 days
     -------------------------------------------------------------------------
     WHY THIS MATTERS:
     In a traditional manual QA workflow a QA analyst must:
       (a) Locate and play the recorded call (~2–5 min)
       (b) Listen end-to-end while filling in a scoring form (~15–20 min)
       (c) Write coaching notes and submit (~5–10 min)
     This creates a bottleneck when call volumes exceed analyst capacity.

     HOW THE PLATFORM ACHIEVES IT:
       - Audio is automatically transcribed by Google Cloud Speech-to-Text
         (typically < 60 seconds for a 10-minute call).
       - Google Gemini 1.5 Pro scores every parameter in the evaluation form
         against the transcript, with a reasoning note per score, in < 90
         seconds.
       - The analyst sees a pre-filled review form and needs only to
         accept / adjust / reject AI suggestions — not fill in from scratch.
       - RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) enriches the prompt with
         relevant policy excerpts, so the AI can handle specialist parameters
         that would otherwise require the analyst to cross-reference manuals.

     HOW IT IS MEASURED:
       - AuditLog table timestamps:
           * UploadedAt   → when the transcript or audio was submitted
           * EvaluatedAt  → when the AI completed scoring
           * ReviewedAt   → when the human analyst submitted the final review
       - Analytics API computes: ReviewedAt – UploadedAt = Total QA time
       - GCP Cloud Monitoring tracks P50 / P95 pipeline latency over time.
       - Target: median Total QA time < 5 minutes at the 90-day checkpoint.

     ANALYTICS EVIDENCE (where to look):
       - Main Analytics Dashboard → Daily Score Trend chart shows throughput
         growth (more evaluations per day = less time per evaluation).
       - Decision Assurance → Section Calibration shows whether time savings
         correlate with maintained or improved scoring quality.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1.2  Achieve >= 0.85 correlation between AI scores and human-reviewed scores
     -------------------------------------------------------------------------
     WHY THIS MATTERS:
     A Pearson correlation of >= 0.85 between AI-suggested scores and
     analyst-adjusted scores means the AI is a reliable first-pass scorer.
     Below 0.85, analysts are overriding too often, negating time savings
     and introducing doubt about AI reliability.

     HOW IT IS MEASURED:
       - HumanReview module records both the AI-suggested score and the
         human-approved score for every parameter.
       - The Analytics API computes Pearson r across all reviewed
         parameter-level scores (AI score vs human score pairs).
       - A rolling 30-day window is tracked so regression after model
         updates is detected early.

     HOW IT IS SURFACED (HITL Comparison Analytics — see Section 3.4):
       - AI vs Human Comparison → Section Comparison bar chart: shows the
         average AI score % vs average human score % per form section.
       - AI vs Human Comparison → Parameter Comparison table: drills into
         individual parameters where AI and human diverge most, flagging
         those with a large Difference value (Human % – AI %).
       - Explainability → HITL Agreement chart: shows the % of reviews where
         analyst chose Agree / Disagree / Partial for each sampling policy.

     RISK SIGNAL:
       - If correlation drops below 0.85, the Decision Assurance → Risk Radar
         may flag affected parameters under the "DecisionReversal" category.
       - Gemini model version is pinned in AiConfig to prevent silent
         regression after model updates.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1.3  Onboard 80% of targeted QA analysts within 3 months
     -------------------------------------------------------
     WHY THIS MATTERS:
     A powerful platform that is not adopted delivers no value. Tracking
     active adoption ensures training and change management are working.

     HOW IT IS MEASURED:
       - AppUsers table: role = QA Analyst, IsActive = true.
       - AuditLog table: any analyst with at least one ReviewedAt record
         within the 90-day window is counted as "active".
       - Adoption rate = active analysts / total licensed analyst seats.
       - Tracked weekly; escalated to TechM GVO team in weekly review meeting
         if adoption lags behind the 80% target trajectory.

     SUPPORTING ANALYTICS:
       - TNI Dashboard (see Section 3.5) shows per-analyst training plan
         completion rates — low completion may predict low adoption.
       - Insights Chat can be queried: "Which analysts have not submitted
         a review in the last two weeks?" to identify at-risk users.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1.4  Zero PII data leakage incidents post go-live
     ------------------------------------------------
     WHY THIS MATTERS:
     Calls contain customer names, account numbers, addresses, and
     potentially payment card data. A single leakage incident can trigger
     GDPR fines, loss of customer trust, and contract penalties.

     HOW IT IS PREVENTED:
       - PiiRedactionService (powered by Gemini) masks PII entities in
         the transcript before any data is written to the database.
       - Entities masked include: names, phone numbers, email addresses,
         account IDs, dates of birth, payment card numbers (PCI-DSS).
       - Audio files are never persisted to the database — only the
         redacted text transcript is stored.
       - Google API keys are stored encrypted in the AiConfigs table or
         OS environment variables; never in source code.

     HOW IT IS MEASURED:
       - AuditLog table: PiiRedacted = true/false recorded per call.
       - Manual spot-check sample: compliance team reviews 20 random
         transcripts per month to verify redaction accuracy.
       - Target: >= 99% PII redaction accuracy (verified by spot-check).
       - Incident tracking: any confirmed PII leakage reported to the
         Security team and escalated to GVO within 24 hours.

     RISK SIGNAL:
       - Decision Assurance → Risk Radar may flag a "BiasIndicator" if
         a parameter consistently produces anomalous low scores on calls
         where PII redaction removed key phrases, indicating the AI
         may be making decisions based on removed tokens.

SECTION 2 — MEASUREMENT (System-Level Analytics)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

2.1  AuditLog table — tracks every action
     -----------------------------------------
     WHAT IT RECORDS:
       - Entity type (Audit, CallRecord, EvaluationForm, KnowledgeSource, etc.)
       - Action type (Create, Update, Delete, Review, Export, Login)
       - Timestamp (UTC)
       - Actor (UserId + username)
       - Project context (ProjectId)
       - Before/after snapshot for update actions

     HOW IT FEEDS ANALYTICS:
       - Analytics API aggregates AuditLog entries to compute:
           * QA Analyst throughput: audit count per analyst per day
           * Time-to-review: ReviewedAt – UploadedAt per call
           * Session frequency: login events per analyst per week
       - AuditLog is immutable (append-only); no Update/Delete on the log
         itself — ensuring a tamper-proof compliance trail.
       - Available via Insights Chat: natural-language queries like "How
         many calls did Agent X review last week?" are answered using
         AuditLog aggregations.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

2.2  HumanReview module — AI vs human score diff
     ------------------------------------------------
     WHAT IT CAPTURES:
       - For each evaluated parameter:
           * AiScore     (raw Gemini-suggested score)
           * HumanScore  (analyst-approved score)
           * AiReasoning (Gemini's explanation of the score)
           * HumanNote   (analyst's override justification, optional)
           * ReviewVerdict: Agree | Disagree | Partial
       - Stored in HumanFieldScores table, linked to the parent Audit record.

     WHY THE DIFF MATTERS:
       - Systematic disagreements (e.g., Gemini always over-scores
         "Compliance Disclosure") point to prompt engineering gaps or
         knowledge-base deficiencies, not random error.
       - These gaps feed directly into sprint retrospectives and model
         improvement backlog.

     HOW THE DIFF IS SURFACED:
       - HITL Comparison Analytics: Section and Parameter level side-by-side
         bar charts (AI % vs Human %).
       - Explainability Analytics: HITL Agreement doughnut chart showing
         Agree / Disagree / Partial split by sampling policy.
       - Decision Assurance: Risk Radar flags parameters with high
         DecisionReversal rate (frequent human overrides).

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

2.3  GCP Cloud Monitoring — uptime, latency, error rate
     ------------------------------------------------------
     WHAT IS MONITORED:
       - /api/ping health endpoint (uptime check, 1-minute interval)
       - API response time (P50, P95, P99 latencies)
       - Error rate: HTTP 5xx responses per minute
       - Gemini API call latency and error rate (429 / 500)
       - Google Cloud STT call latency and error rate
       - SQL Server query duration (via custom metrics from the app)
       - VM CPU / memory / disk utilisation on the e2-standard-4 instance

     ALERTING:
       - Uptime check failure → PagerDuty / email alert within 2 minutes.
       - P95 latency > 10 seconds → warning alert.
       - Error rate > 5% over 5 minutes → critical alert.
       - Disk utilisation > 80% → warning alert (guards against large
         transcript accumulation).

     RELATIONSHIP TO OUTCOME METRICS:
       - If P95 pipeline latency exceeds 5 minutes the "< 5 min QA time"
         target is at risk — Cloud Monitoring provides early warning.
       - GCP dashboards are linked from the weekly TechM GVO review pack.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

2.4  User satisfaction survey (30 / 60 / 90-day checkpoints)
     ------------------------------------------------------------
     SURVEY STRUCTURE:
       - Distributed at day 30, 60, and 90 of the pilot to all QA analysts
         and QA managers using the platform.
       - Questions cover:
           * Overall satisfaction (NPS: 0–10)
           * Time saved per evaluation (perceived, vs. the measured metric)
           * Trust in AI scores (1–5 Likert)
           * Ease of overriding AI scores (1–5 Likert)
           * Usefulness of coaching recommendations (1–5 Likert)
           * Top 3 pain points (open text)
           * Top 3 requested improvements (open text)

     HOW RESULTS FEED BACK:
       - Survey results are reviewed in the monthly GVO steering meeting.
       - Low trust scores (<3/5) trigger a sprint to improve AI reasoning
         explainability and coaching note quality.
       - Pain points are triaged into the product backlog and communicated
         back to participants within 1 week of the survey close.

SECTION 3 — ANALYTICS FEATURES — DETAILED EXPLAINABILITY
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

3.1  Main Analytics Dashboard
     --------------------------
     CHARTS AND THEIR MEANING:

     a) Daily QA Score Trend (Line Chart)
        - X-axis: date; Y-axis: average QA score % across all audits that day.
        - Data source: DailyScores (average of all EvaluationScores per day,
          keyed by CallDate or EvaluatedAt).
        - WHY IT MATTERS: A sudden drop in the daily average may indicate a
          new evaluation form that is harder to score, a change in call
          quality, or an AI model version change.
        - AI-GENERATED INSIGHT: DailyTrendInsight — Gemini analyses the
          trend and returns a 2–3 sentence narrative (e.g., "Scores improved
          steadily from 72% to 81% over the last 30 days, consistent with
          increased adoption and analyst familiarity with the review workflow.
          No significant dips were observed following the v2 form rollout.").

     b) Agent Performance Bar Chart
        - Shows average QA score % per agent, sorted descending.
        - Data source: AgentScores.
        - WHY IT MATTERS: Identifies high performers and agents at risk,
          feeding the TNI (training needs) workflow.
        - AI-GENERATED INSIGHT: AgentPerformanceInsight.

     c) Parameter Trend Chart (Horizontal Bar)
        - Shows average score % per evaluation parameter (top 15 by volume).
        - Data source: ParameterTrends.
        - WHY IT MATTERS: Pinpoints which specific QA criteria are
          consistently missed across all agents — a systemic coaching gap
          rather than an individual one.
        - AI-GENERATED INSIGHT: ParameterInsight.

     d) Call Type Score Chart (Doughnut / Radar)
        - Compares average QA score % per evaluation form (e.g., Sales call,
          Complaint call, Technical support call).
        - Data source: CallTypeScores.
        - WHY IT MATTERS: Some call types are inherently harder to score
          well. This chart prevents unfair agent comparisons across
          different call types.
        - AI-GENERATED INSIGHT: CallTypeInsight.

     e) Agent Daily Trend (Multi-line Chart)
        - One line per agent; X-axis = date; Y-axis = daily avg score %.
        - Data source: AgentDailyTrends.
        - WHY IT MATTERS: Shows whether an individual agent's improvement
          or decline is a short-term blip or a sustained trend.

     f) Section Score Overview (Radar Chart)
        - Per-section average score % plotted on a radar/spider chart.
        - Data source: SectionScores.
        - WHY IT MATTERS: Immediately shows which sections of the evaluation
          form (e.g., "Opening", "Compliance", "Resolution") are weakest
          across the whole team.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

3.2  Explainability Analytics
     --------------------------
     PURPOSE: Explains WHY audits pass or fail by surfacing which parameters
     drove outcomes — moving beyond "what the score is" to "why it is that".

     CHARTS AND THEIR MEANING:

     a) Decision Drivers Table / Bar Chart
        - Lists each evaluation parameter with:
            * Average score %
            * LowScoreCount (audits where this parameter scored < 60%)
            * HighScoreCount (audits where this parameter scored >= 80%)
            * ScoreVariability (standard deviation of scores)
            * IsRiskArea flag (average < 60%)
        - WHY IT MATTERS: Parameters with high variability AND high
          LowScoreCount are systemic failure drivers — they need policy
          clarification or additional knowledge-base content so Gemini
          can score them more consistently.
        - AI-GENERATED INSIGHT: DecisionDriversInsight.

     b) AI vs Human Agreement (HITL Agreement) Doughnut
        - Shows split of reviewer verdicts: Agree / Disagree / Partial.
        - Broken down by sampling policy name.
        - WHY IT MATTERS: A high Disagree rate on a specific sampling policy
          may mean the policy selects calls that are atypical and harder for
          the AI to score (e.g., escalation calls selected at a higher rate).
        - AI-GENERATED INSIGHT: HitlAgreementInsight.

     c) Signal Utilisation Chart (Stacked Bar)
        - Per parameter: FullScoreRate vs MissRate.
        - FullScoreRate: % of times the agent achieved maximum marks.
        - MissRate: % of times the parameter scored zero.
        - WHY IT MATTERS: A parameter with a 60% MissRate is a training
          priority. It also validates whether the AI's scoring is
          discriminating (low MissRate = AI may be too lenient).
        - AI-GENERATED INSIGHT: SignalUsageInsight.

     d) Failure Reason Analysis Table
        - Ranks parameters by FailedAuditCount (how many failed audits
          this parameter contributed to) and ContributionPercent.
        - WHY IT MATTERS: Gives QA managers a prioritised list of coaching
          interventions with the highest expected impact on pass rates.
        - AI-GENERATED INSIGHT: FailureReasonsInsight.

     OVERALL EXPLAINABILITY METRICS (page header):
       - TotalAudits: total evaluations in the analysis window.
       - TotalReviewed: evaluations that have gone through human review.
       - AiHitlAgreementRate: overall % of reviewed parameters where
         the analyst selected "Agree" (AI score accepted unchanged).

3.3  Decision Assurance Analytics (Advanced)
     ------------------------------------------
     PURPOSE: Provides a higher-order view of decision quality, consistency,
     and risk — answering "Can we trust the decisions this platform is
     making, and where are the highest-risk gaps?"

     CHARTS AND THEIR MEANING:

     a) Decision Confidence Score Chart (Horizontal Bar)
        - Per parameter:
            * AvgScorePercent: mean score across all audits
            * ConsistencyScore: 1 – (StdDev / MaxPossible)
              → 1.0 = always scored the same; 0 = completely random
            * ConfidenceScore: AvgScorePercent × ConsistencyScore / 100
              → composite; high = consistently good; low = inconsistent
            * RiskLevel: High / Medium / Low
        - WHY IT MATTERS: A parameter can have a high average score but
          low confidence (inconsistent — sometimes perfect, sometimes zero).
          That inconsistency is a risk because decisions cannot be relied
          upon. Conversely, a consistently low score is a known, manageable
          training gap.

     b) Agent Risk Profile Table
        - Per agent:
            * RecentAvgPercent: average score % in the last 30 days
            * PriorAvgPercent: average score % in the 30 days before that
            * Momentum: RecentAvg – PriorAvg
              → Positive = improving; negative = declining
            * Trend: Improving / Stable / Declining
            * RiskLevel: High / Medium / Low
        - WHY IT MATTERS: An agent with a Declining trend and High risk
          level should be prioritised for coaching before their metrics
          cause customer-impact. This proactive flagging replaces reactive
          spot-check-based identification.

     c) Section Calibration Chart (Grouped Bar + Confusion Score)
        - Per evaluation section:
            * RecentAvgPercent vs PriorAvgPercent (side-by-side bars)
            * ConfusionScore (0–100): derived from StdDev relative to mean
              → High confusion = analysts/AI are interpreting the section
                inconsistently (policy is unclear or AI prompt needs work)
            * DriftDirection: Improving / Stable / Declining / Volatile
        - WHY IT MATTERS: Section-level confusion scores are actionable at
          the QA team management level — they identify which sections of
          the evaluation form need policy rewrites, training sessions, or
          updated knowledge-base documents.

     d) *** RISK RADAR *** (Highlighted Feature)
        ------------------------------------------
        WHAT IS THE RISK RADAR?
        The Risk Radar is the platform's highest-level early-warning system.
        It scans all evaluation parameters and flags items that exhibit one
        or more of four specific risk patterns, assigning each a risk score
        from 0 to 100.

        RISK CATEGORIES (what each one means):

        i.  PolicyConfusion
            SIGNAL: High score variability (large standard deviation) on a
            parameter across audits — the same behaviour is being scored
            very differently in different calls.
            ROOT CAUSE: Unclear policy wording, ambiguous scoring guidance,
            or insufficient knowledge-base documentation for that parameter.
            ACTION: QA Manager reviews the parameter definition; knowledge-
            base document is updated; Gemini prompt is refined.

        ii. EscalationRisk
            SIGNAL: A parameter that strongly correlates with call escalations
            or negative sentiment, AND has a high MissRate or declining trend.
            ROOT CAUSE: Agents are not following the specific compliance step
            (e.g., "Offer callback") that prevents escalations.
            ACTION: Targeted coaching; the parameter is elevated to P0 in
            the evaluation form; sampling rate for this call type is increased.

        iii. BiasIndicator
            SIGNAL: A parameter where the AI score and human score differ
            consistently in one direction (AI always higher or always lower
            than human) for a specific agent or call type.
            ROOT CAUSE: The AI may have a systematic bias based on transcript
            patterns (e.g., always rewarding formal language even when the
            required behaviour was absent). Alternatively, a human analyst
            may have a personal bias in scoring.
            ACTION: The parameter's scoring rubric is reviewed; golden
            transcript regression tests are added; the affected analyst
            may receive calibration coaching.

        iv. DecisionReversal
            SIGNAL: A high rate of human overrides (Disagree / Partial) for a
            specific parameter — the analyst frequently rejects the AI score.
            ROOT CAUSE: The AI's reasoning for that parameter is not aligned
            with how analysts interpret the policy in practice.
            ACTION: The parameter's prompt context is updated; additional
            RAG knowledge chunks are added; the model's output is reviewed
            for reasoning quality.

        HOW RISK SCORE (0–100) IS COMPUTED:
          - PolicyConfusion:    Risk Score = min(100, ScoreStdDev × 5)
          - EscalationRisk:     Risk Score = MissRate × 100
          - BiasIndicator:      Risk Score = abs(AvgAiScore – AvgHumanScore)
                                             / MaxRating × 100
          - DecisionReversal:   Risk Score = DisagreeRate × 100
          Items with RiskScore >= 40 appear in the Risk Radar panel.

        HOW IT IS DISPLAYED:
          - A table of risk items, sortable by RiskScore descending.
          - Each row: Parameter, Section, Risk Category, Risk Score (0–100),
            and a plain-English Description generated by the analytics engine.
          - Colour-coded severity: Red (>= 70), Amber (40–69), Green (< 40).
          - Feeds directly into the sprint planning backlog:
            Red items → immediate action; Amber → next sprint.

     e) Calibration Heatmap
        - A grid: parameters (rows) × agents (columns).
        - Cell value: agent's average score % for that parameter.
        - AgentSpread: Max – Min across agents for that parameter.
        - High spread (e.g., >30%) means different agents score the same
          parameter very differently — calibration sessions needed.
        - WHY IT MATTERS: The heatmap makes cross-agent inconsistency visible
          at a glance. A parameter that one agent scores at 90% and another
          at 40% is not a quality problem — it is a calibration problem.

3.4  AI vs Human (HITL) Comparison Analytics
     --------------------------------------------
     PURPOSE: Side-by-side comparison of Gemini AI scores vs human analyst
     scores at section and parameter level.

     CHARTS:
       a) Section Comparison (Grouped Bar)
          - Per section: AI avg % (blue) vs Human avg % (orange).
          - Difference column: Human% – AI% (positive = human rates higher).
          - SampleCount: number of reviewed parameters in this section.

       b) Parameter Comparison (Data Table)
          - Per parameter: MaxRating, AvgAiScore, AvgHumanScore,
            AvgAiScorePercent, AvgHumanScorePercent, Difference, SampleCount.
          - Sortable by Difference to find biggest AI-Human gaps quickly.

     HOW THIS SUPPORTS THE >= 0.85 CORRELATION TARGET:
       - If section-level differences are consistently large (> 10%),
         the overall Pearson r is likely below target.
       - Drilling into the parameter table identifies the specific
         parameters pulling down the correlation score.
       - Actions: update knowledge-base content, refine Gemini prompt
         for that parameter, or re-run calibration with the analyst team.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

3.5  TNI (Training Needs Identification) Dashboard
     -------------------------------------------------
     PURPOSE: Tracks training plan creation, progress, and completion for
     each agent, auto-generated from QA scoring data.

     KEY METRICS:
       - TotalPlans: all active training plans in the project.
       - OpenPlans: plans in Draft / Active / InProgress status.
       - CompletedPlans: plans marked Completed or Closed.
       - OverduePlans: plans whose DueDate has passed and are not closed.

     BY AGENT VIEW:
       - Per agent: TotalPlans, OpenPlans, CompletedPlans, CompletionRate %.
       - Agents with low CompletionRate AND high RiskLevel (from Agent Risk
         Profile) are the highest-priority coaching interventions.

     AUTO-GENERATION LINK:
       - When Call Pipeline finishes and QA scores are below threshold,
         TniGenerationService creates a training plan automatically, listing
         the specific parameters the agent missed and suggested resources.
       - IsAutoGenerated flag distinguishes these from manually created plans.

     RELATIONSHIP TO ADOPTION TARGET:
       - TNI completion rate is a leading indicator of adoption.
         Analysts who engage with their training plans are more likely to
         actively use the platform's review features.

SECTION 4 — QUALITATIVE FEEDBACK MECHANISMS
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

4.1  QA analyst interviews at end of each sprint
     -----------------------------------------------
     FREQUENCY: End of each 3-week sprint (approx. every 3 weeks).
     FORMAT: 30-minute structured interview with 2–3 QA analysts.
     TOPICS:
       - What did you find easiest / hardest about using the platform?
       - Were AI-suggested scores helpful or misleading?
       - Did the coaching notes improve your feedback to agents?
       - What feature would most improve your daily workflow?
     OUTPUT: Sprint retrospective action items; input to product backlog.

     ANALYTICS LINKAGE:
       - Interview insights are cross-referenced with analytics data.
         E.g., if an analyst says "I always disagree with the Compliance
         score", the HITL Comparison chart is pulled to verify whether
         this is statistically significant or an individual perception.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

4.2  In-app feedback form (linked from Review page)
     ---------------------------------------------------
     LOCATION: "Give Feedback" button on the Human Review and Call Pipeline
     results pages.
     CAPTURES:
       - Overall usefulness of AI suggestions (1–5 stars)
       - Was the AI reasoning clear? (Yes / Partially / No)
       - Free-text comment (optional)
       - Page context (auto-captured: which review form, which call type)
     STORAGE: FeedbackSubmissions table (if implemented) or exported to
     a shared spreadsheet reviewed weekly.
     RESPONSE SLA: Product team acknowledges actionable feedback within
     5 business days.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

4.3  Weekly review meeting — TechM GVO team
     -------------------------------------------
     CADENCE: Every Monday, 45 minutes.
     ATTENDEES: QA Automation Engineering lead, TechM GVO team lead,
                QA Manager (pilot team), Product Owner.
     STANDING AGENDA:
       1. Review GCP Cloud Monitoring dashboard (uptime, latency, errors)
       2. Review Analytics Dashboard (throughput, score trends)
       3. Review Risk Radar — any Red items require an owner and due date
       4. Adoption rate vs 80% target trajectory
       5. User satisfaction survey results (at checkpoint weeks)
       6. Sprint demo of new features (sprint boundary weeks)
       7. Blockers and escalations
     OUTPUT: Action log updated in shared project tracker; blockers
     escalated same day if customer-impacting.

     WHY THE RISK RADAR IS A STANDING AGENDA ITEM:
       - The Risk Radar aggregates signals that would otherwise require
         manually reviewing multiple analytics tabs. By surfacing
         PolicyConfusion, EscalationRisk, BiasIndicator, and
         DecisionReversal items in one ranked table, the weekly meeting
         can prioritise the highest-impact actions in under 10 minutes.
